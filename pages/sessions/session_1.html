Session Cases

Thyroid Nodule Evaluation
Follicular Lesion Assessment

Clinical Information

45-year-old female with a 2.5 cm solitary thyroid nodule discovered on routine physical examination. Ultrasound shows a hypoechoic nodule with microcalcifications. Serum TSH is within normal limits. Fine needle aspiration performed.

Adrenal Mass
Cortical vs. Medullary Origin

Clinical Information

38-year-old male with hypertension and episodic palpitations. CT scan reveals a 4 cm right adrenal mass with heterogeneous enhancement. Elevated 24-hour urinary catecholamines and metanephrines. Surgical resection performed.

Pituitary Mass
Functional Classification

Clinical Information

29-year-old female with amenorrhea and galactorrhea for 8 months. MRI shows a 1.2 cm sellar mass with suprasellar extension. Elevated serum prolactin (180 ng/mL). Transsphenoidal resection performed due to visual field defects.

Parathyroid Pathology
Hyperplasia vs. Adenoma
